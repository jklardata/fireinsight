Compliance Requirements

The 6 Mandatory NERIS Modules

Every incident must include complete data from all six modules. Missing any mandatory field makes the record non-compliant.

01

Core Incident

Fundamental identification and timing for every incident.

Incident ID Incident type Alarm time Dispatch time Arrival time Cleared time Incident status
02

Location / Geocoding

Standardized address with mandatory GPS coordinates — a major change from NFIRS.

Street address City / state / ZIP GPS lat/lon (required)
03

Life Safety Outcomes

Injury and fatality data for civilians and firefighters. Zeros must be explicitly recorded — blank is non-compliant.

Civilian fatalities Civilian injuries FF fatalities FF injuries
04

Actions & Tactics

What was done at the scene. The most frequently skipped module in practice.

Primary action taken
05

Fire Module

Required for all fire-type incidents. Captures cause, origin, and building characteristics. Fire only

Area of origin Heat source Construction type
06

Aid Classification

Whether mutual aid was given or received, and to/from which agency.

Aid given / received Assisting agency
Where We Are

NERIS Rollout Timeline

The transition from NFIRS to NERIS has been underway for several years. Here's where the program stands.

2022 — 2024
NERIS Development & Pilot Program
FSRI developed NERIS in collaboration with the fire service. Pilot departments tested the data model, API, and RMS integrations across multiple states.
January 1, 2025
NFIRS Officially Retired
The NFIRS system was officially decommissioned as the federal reporting standard. Departments began transitioning to NERIS submission workflows.
January 1, 2026 — NOW
NERIS Compliance Required
Full NERIS compliance is now required for all reporting departments. FEMA grant programs reference NERIS data quality in applicant scoring. Departments not yet compliant are at grant risk.
2026 — Ongoing
EMS Module & Wildland Module Rollout
FSRI is finalizing expanded modules for EMS incidents and wildland fire data. Departments in WUI areas and high EMS volume departments should begin preparing field collection now.
2027 Grant Cycle
NERIS Data Quality Becomes Formal Grant Criterion
Anticipated tightening of NERIS data quality requirements in AFG and SAFER scoring. Departments with multi-year clean NERIS records will have a significant competitive advantage.

The major RMS vendors — ESO, FIREHOUSE, and ImageTrend — all have NERIS modules available. Important: having the module available doesn't mean it's turned on or configured correctly. Contact your vendor to verify that NERIS fields are being collected and exported in the correct format.
Outright disqualification is not yet the universal standard, but the consequences are already real. Grant reviewers use NERIS data to assess applicant need — incomplete or non-compliant data weakens your score on need-based criteria. Future grant cycles are expected to formalize this as a hard requirement.
Based on real-world compliance audits, the most common gaps are:
  • Missing GPS coordinates — many departments collect address but not lat/lon
  • Incomplete timestamps — dispatch time and cleared time frequently missing
  • No primary action recorded — the Actions & Tactics module is often skipped on fast calls
  • Life safety fields left blank — "no injuries" must be recorded as zero, not omitted
  • Fire module incomplete — area of origin and heat source often missing on structure fires
Yes, but with limitations. A conversion tool can map NFIRS fields to NERIS fields for historical data. However, NFIRS lacks several NERIS mandatory fields — particularly GPS coordinates and some life safety outcome fields. Converted records will have known gaps. You cannot achieve full NERIS compliance from NFIRS data alone; you need to update your collection process going forward. FireInsight includes an NFIRS-to-NERIS conversion tool in the app.
Training alone is rarely enough. The most effective approach combines three things: (1) configure your RMS to require mandatory fields before submission — make incomplete records un-submissible, (2) run a monthly data quality report and share it with company officers so crews see the gaps, and (3) tie completion rates to performance feedback. Crew-level ownership is the single biggest driver of improvement.

NERIS uses an API-first architecture. Departments submit incident data via a REST API rather than file uploads. Most departments won't interact with the API directly — your RMS vendor handles submission automatically. If you're submitting directly or building an integration, you'll need OAuth2 credentials (Client ID and Client Secret) from the USFA/FSRI portal.
